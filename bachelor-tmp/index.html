Bachelor Thesis Overview

Prog Gen

Title: Exploring different ways to increase immersion in procedurally generated 2D game worlds

Goal: Build a small game and implement different proc gen algorithms and analyse impact on player immersion.

  • Gameplay very simple, something like resource gathering and crafting
  • The more structure a system has the harder it is to procedurally generate it
  • Nature fairly easy to generate, human structures harder

In nature here are basically no interdependencies. See how a single path (non natural) is already much harder to generate procedurally.

Streets of Rogue, proc gen of an entire world, see https://youtu.be/nGahQVeBoTY?t=128. Much more challenging.

NPCs

Title: Comparing the impact on immersion between minimalistic and sophisticated NPCs

Goal: Create a simple sandbox game with the focus on minimalistic yet immersive NPCs.

  • The lower the player expectations towards NPC behaviour, the easier it is to keep player immersed
  • Very much influenced by art direction and level of realism
  • NPCs can seem much more intelligent than they actually are with simple tricks
  • Explore ways to utilize these tricks and avoid immersion breaking pitfalls

Rain World has as its primary focus NPCs/enemy interaction. The individual AI is not all that complex, but it appears smart because it doesn't behave in unexpected ways (most of the enemies are animals).

Skyrim has a lot of interaction, however that means that it's extremely hard to account for every last edge case. You can freely rob any NPC by placing a bucket on their had because they can't see you.

Idler Game

Title: Increasing UX and perceived meaningfulness in Idler games by combining it with the Building genre

Goal: Create a small game about planting crops and trees and building your own garden/natural habitat.

  • Most idler games are literally just number go up
  • While there is a market for that (seemingly), I believe these games can be greatly improved in regard to how meaningful they feel
  • Combine the gratification of progress with the creative outlet of building
  • Plants interact with eachother and reward/punish if other plants of a certain type are placed closely

Game would be 2D top down pixel art game (below is the game Terrafactor as an idea).

ISLANDERS uses score points as reward/punishment for building. Some buildings have positive effects towards others negative effects.
